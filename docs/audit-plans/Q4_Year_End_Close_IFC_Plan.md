## PART A: FINANCIAL STATEMENT CLOSE PROCESS (FSCP)

**Lead**: TM1

### OBJECTIVES

1. Verify year-end closing procedures followed per FSCP SOP
2. Ensure all balance sheet accounts reconciled
3. Validate provisions and accruals are complete and accurate
4. Confirm revenue and expense cut-off is appropriate
5. Assess MIS reporting accuracy and timeliness
6. Verify Chart of Accounts management
7. Ensure books properly closed and locked

### SCOPE

**In-Scope Activities:**
1. Closing schedule adherence
2. Balance confirmations (AR, AP)
3. Account reconciliations (all key accounts)
4. Provisions and accruals
5. Unbilled and deferred revenue
6. Bank reconciliations (year-end)
7. Forex revaluation (if applicable)
8. Manual journal entries review
9. MIS reporting
10. Books closure and locking

### CONTROL TESTING

#### Test FC1: Closing Schedule Adherence
**Objective**: Verify finance team followed closing timeline

**Procedure:**
1. Obtain FSCP SOP closing schedule
2. Verify key milestones:
   - 25th March: Initiation of close, vendor invoice requests
   - 30th March: Last day transactions
   - 5th April: Balance confirmations sent
   - 10th April: Reconciliations completed
   - 15th April: Provisions and adjustments
   - 20th April: Books finalized
3. Obtain evidence for each milestone:
   - Emails, meeting notes, task completion logs
4. Assess if timeline was met
5. Investigate any delays and impact

**Workpaper**: Q4-FSC-01

**Expected Evidence:**
- FSCP SOP
- Closing schedule with completion dates
- Email communications
- Task completion logs

#### Test FC2: Balance Confirmations - Accounts Receivable
**Objective**: Verify AR balances confirmed with customers

**Procedure:**
1. Obtain list of customers with balances as of Mar 31, 2026
2. Verify balance confirmation requests sent (per FSCP, by Apr 5)
3. Sample 10 major customers:
   - Verify confirmation request sent
   - Check if response received
   - If response received:
     * Compare confirmed balance with books
     * Investigate any differences
     * Verify Finance Manager reviewed reconciliation
   - If no response:
     * Verify follow-up attempts
     * Perform alternative procedures (subsequent receipts)

**Workpaper**: Q4-FSC-02

**Expected Evidence:**
- Customer balance confirmation requests
- Confirmation responses
- Reconciliation worksheets
- Finance Manager review

#### Test FC3: Balance Confirmations - Accounts Payable
**Objective**: Verify AP balances confirmed with vendors

**Procedure:**
1. Obtain list of vendors with balances as of Mar 31, 2026
2. Sample 10 major vendors:
   - Verify confirmation request sent or vendor statement obtained
   - Compare vendor statement with books
   - Investigate any differences
   - Verify Finance Manager reviewed reconciliation
3. Check for any old credit balances:
   - Investigate nature
   - Assess if refund due or adjustment needed

**Workpaper**: Q4-FSC-03

**Expected Evidence:**
- Vendor statements
- Reconciliation worksheets
- Finance Manager review

#### Test FC4: Bank Reconciliation - Year-End
**Objective**: Ensure year-end bank reconciliations are accurate

**Procedure:**
1. For all bank accounts as of Mar 31, 2026:
   - Obtain bank reconciliation statement
   - Verify prepared by Finance Executive
   - Verify reviewed by Finance Manager
   - Recalculate reconciliation
   - Review reconciling items:
     * Deposits in transit (should clear in Apr)
     * Outstanding cheques (should clear in Apr)
     * Bank charges/interest not recorded
     * Any unusual items
2. Verify subsequent clearance (Apr bank statement)
3. Investigate any long-outstanding items (>30 days)
4. Assess if any provisions needed

**Workpaper**: Q4-FSC-04

**Expected Evidence:**
- Bank reconciliation statements
- Bank statements (Mar and Apr)
- Tally bank ledgers
- Finance Manager review

#### Test FC5: Provisions and Accruals
**Objective**: Verify completeness and accuracy of year-end provisions

**Procedure:**
1. Obtain list of all provisions and accruals as of Mar 31, 2026
2. Assess completeness:
   - Audit fees
   - Electricity/utilities (if bill not received)
   - Rent (if applicable)
   - Bonuses/incentives
   - Leave encashment
   - Gratuity (if applicable)
   - Professional fees
   - Any other known liabilities
3. Sample 10 significant provisions:
   - Verify basis of calculation
   - Recalculate amount
   - Check supporting documentation
   - Verify management approval
   - Check TDS deducted (if applicable)
   - Verify accounting entry in Tally
4. Test subsequent realization (Apr-May):
   - Check if actual invoice received
   - Compare actual vs provision
   - Assess reasonableness of estimate

**Workpaper**: Q4-FSC-05 to Q4-FSC-06

**Expected Evidence:**
- Provision schedule
- Calculation worksheets
- Management approval
- Tally entries
- Subsequent invoices

## PART B: INTERNAL FINANCIAL CONTROLS (IFC) EVALUATION

**Lead**: TM3

### OBJECTIVES

1. Assess effectiveness of IFC over financial reporting for FY25-26
2. Summarize control testing results from H1, Q3, and Q4 audits
3. Identify control deficiencies and assess severity
4. Evaluate fraud risk controls
5. Assess IT general controls
6. Provide overall IFC opinion for management certification

### SCOPE

**In-Scope Control Areas:**
1. Revenue and Order-to-Cash
2. Procure-to-Pay and Vendor Management
3. Treasury and Cash Management
4. Taxation and Statutory Compliance
5. Payroll and HR
6. Fixed Assets
7. Financial Statement Close Process
8. IT General Controls (Tally)
9. Fraud Risk Controls
10. Management Review Controls

### IFC TESTING

#### Test IFC1: IFC Control Matrix Preparation
**Objective**: Document all key financial reporting controls

**Procedure:**
1. Compile list of all key controls from:
   - H1 audit (Revenue, P2P, Treasury, Tax)
   - Q3 audit (Payroll, HR)
   - Q4 audit (Fixed Assets, FSCP)
2. For each control, document:
   - Control ID
   - Control description
   - Control owner
   - Control frequency
   - Control type (preventive/detective)
   - Risk addressed
   - Test performed (reference to workpaper)
   - Test result (Effective/Ineffective)
   - Deficiency noted (if any)
3. Prepare comprehensive IFC control matrix

**Workpaper**: Q4-IFC-01

**Expected Evidence:**
- IFC control matrix
- Cross-references to all audit workpapers

#### Test IFC2: Control Testing Results Summary
**Objective**: Summarize results of all control tests

**Procedure:**
1. Review all control test results from:
   - H1 audit workpapers
   - Q3 audit workpapers
   - Q4 audit workpapers
2. For each control:
   - Summarize test performed
   - Document sample size
   - Note exceptions found
   - Assess control effectiveness
3. Categorize controls:
   - Effective (no exceptions or minor exceptions)
   - Ineffective (significant exceptions)
   - Not tested (if any, with reason)

**Workpaper**: Q4-IFC-02

**Expected Evidence:**
- Control testing summary
- Exception analysis

#### Test IFC3: Control Deficiency Assessment
**Objective**: Evaluate severity of control deficiencies

**Procedure:**
1. Compile all control deficiencies noted during the year
2. For each deficiency:
   - Describe the deficiency
   - Assess likelihood of occurrence
   - Assess magnitude of potential misstatement
   - Classify severity:
     * Material Weakness: Reasonable possibility of material misstatement
     * Significant Deficiency: Important enough to merit attention
     * Deficiency: Less severe but should be addressed
3. Prioritize deficiencies for management action

**Workpaper**: Q4-IFC-03

**Expected Evidence:**
- Deficiency log
- Severity assessment
- Prioritization matrix

#### Test IFC4: Remediation of Prior Findings
**Objective**: Verify H1 and Q3 findings were addressed

**Procedure:**
1. Obtain list of findings from H1 and Q3 audits
2. For each finding:
   - Verify management action plan
   - Check if remediation implemented
   - Test if control now effective
   - Update status (Closed/In Progress/Not Started)
3. Assess if repeat findings indicate systemic issues

**Workpaper**: Q4-IFC-04

**Expected Evidence:**
- Prior audit reports
- Management action plans
- Remediation evidence
- Status update

#### Test IFC5: Fraud Risk Controls
**Objective**: Assess controls to prevent and detect fraud

**Procedure:**
1. Identify key fraud risks:
   - Revenue fraud (fictitious sales, premature recognition)
   - Procurement fraud (kickbacks, fake vendors)
   - Payroll fraud (ghost employees)
   - Asset misappropriation
   - Management override
2. For each fraud risk, assess controls:
   - Preventive controls (segregation of duties, approvals)
   - Detective controls (reconciliations, analytics)
3. Test key fraud risk controls:
   - Segregation of duties (from Test S1)
   - Approval controls (from various tests)
   - Reconciliation controls (from various tests)
   - Analytical review (from various tests)
4. Assess overall fraud risk control environment

**Workpaper**: Q4-IFC-05

**Expected Evidence:**
- Fraud risk assessment
- Fraud control matrix
- Test results

#### Test IFC6: IT General Controls (ITGC)
**Objective**: Assess IT controls supporting financial reporting

**Procedure:**
1. Review Tally ERP controls (from H1 Test S1-S5):
   - User access management
   - Segregation of duties
   - Period locking
   - Audit trail
   - Data backup
2. Assess additional ITGCs:
   - Change management (Tally version updates)
   - Disaster recovery
   - Data security
3. Evaluate reliance on system-generated reports
4. Assess overall ITGC environment

**Workpaper**: Q4-IFC-06

**Expected Evidence:**
- ITGC assessment
- Test results from H1
- Additional ITGC testing (if needed)

#### Test IFC7: Management Review Controls (MRCs)
**Objective**: Assess effectiveness of management oversight

**Procedure:**
1. Identify key management review controls:
   - Monthly MIS review by Directors
   - Budget variance analysis
   - AR/AP aging review
   - Bank reconciliation review
   - Payroll review
   - Compliance calendar review
2. Test effectiveness (from various tests throughout the year)
3. Assess quality and timeliness of reviews
4. Evaluate management's responsiveness to issues

**Workpaper**: Q4-IFC-07

**Expected Evidence:**
- MRC matrix
- Test results
- Management review evidence

#### Test IFC8: Entity-Level Controls (ELCs)
**Objective**: Assess tone at the top and control environment

**Procedure:**
1. Evaluate entity-level controls:
   - Tone at the top (management integrity, ethical values)
   - Organizational structure and authority
   - HR policies (hiring, training, performance)
   - Whistleblower mechanism (if any)
   - Internal audit function
   - Audit committee oversight
2. Conduct interviews with senior management
3. Review code of conduct and ethics policy
4. Assess overall control environment

**Workpaper**: Q4-IFC-08

**Expected Evidence:**
- Interview notes
- Policy documents
- Control environment assessment

#### Test IFC9: Subsequent Events Review
**Objective**: Identify post-year-end events affecting FY25-26

**Procedure:**
1. Review Apr-May 2026 transactions for:
   - Large customer receipts (cut-off issue?)
   - Large vendor payments (accrual issue?)
   - Asset disposals (should have been in FY25-26?)
   - Legal settlements
   - Any other material events
2. Assess impact on FY25-26 financial statements
3. Verify appropriate disclosure or adjustment

**Workpaper**: Q4-IFC-09

**Expected Evidence:**
- Apr-May transaction review
- Impact assessment
- Adjustment/disclosure recommendations

#### Test IFC10: Overall IFC Opinion
**Objective**: Form overall opinion on IFC effectiveness

**Procedure:**
1. Synthesize all IFC testing results
2. Consider:
   - Number and severity of deficiencies
   - Pervasiveness of issues
   - Management's responsiveness
   - Compensating controls
3. Form opinion:
   - Effective (no material weaknesses, minor deficiencies)
   - Effective with exceptions (significant deficiencies but no material weaknesses)
   - Ineffective (material weaknesses identified)
4. Prepare IFC opinion statement for management

**Workpaper**: Q4-IFC-10

**Expected Evidence:**
- IFC opinion memo
- Supporting analysis
