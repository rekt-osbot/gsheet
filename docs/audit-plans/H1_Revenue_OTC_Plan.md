# H1 REVIEW - REVENUE & ORDER-TO-CASH AUDIT PLAN
## SNVA TravelTech Pvt. Ltd. | FY2025-26

**Audit Period**: April - September 2025  
**Fieldwork Period**: October - November 2025  
**Lead Auditor**: Team Member 1 (TM1)  
**Support**: Team Member 3 (TM3) - Data Analytics

---

## OBJECTIVES

1. Verify completeness and accuracy of revenue recognition for H1
2. Ensure all customer orders are billed correctly and timely
3. Confirm accounts receivable are properly recorded and collected
4. Test controls over invoicing, credit notes, and collections
5. Validate compliance with revenue recognition policies and OTC SOP

---

## SCOPE

### In-Scope Revenue Streams
- BPO Services (hourly billing, monthly invoices)
- Ticketing Services (convenience fees, commissions)
- Deposit Incentives
- Any other revenue streams identified

### Key Sub-Processes
1. Customer master setup and maintenance
2. Sales order processing
3. Invoice generation and approval
4. Payment gateway processing
5. Foreign inward remittance handling (FIRC/BRC)
6. Cash/bank reconciliation of collections
7. Credit notes and refunds
8. Revenue cut-off and period-end procedures

---

## RISK ASSESSMENT

### High Risk Areas
- **Foreign currency transactions**: Complex, requires FIRC/BRC verification
- **Revenue cut-off**: Risk of revenue in wrong period (Sept 30 critical)
- **Credit notes/refunds**: Risk of unauthorized revenue reversals
- **Unbilled revenue**: Services delivered but not invoiced

### Medium Risk Areas
- Invoice sequencing and completeness
- Receipt application to invoices
- Long-outstanding receivables

### Low Risk Areas
- Customer master data maintenance
- Standard domestic invoicing

## METHODOLOGY

### Phase 1: Process Understanding (Week 1)
**Activities:**
1. Review OTC SOP in detail
2. Conduct walkthrough with Finance Manager
3. Interview key personnel:
   - Finance Executive (invoicing)
   - Senior Accounts Executive (collections, FIRC)
   - Finance Head (approvals, oversight)
4. Understand revenue streams and billing cycles
5. Identify key controls and control owners
6. Document process flowcharts (if not in SOP)

**Deliverables:**
- Process understanding memo
- Control identification matrix
- Risk assessment worksheet

### Phase 2: Data Collection (Week 2)
**Data Requests to Finance Team:**
1. Sales register from Tally (Apr-Sep 2025) - TM3 to extract
2. List of all invoices with details (date, customer, amount, type)
3. Bank statements for all accounts (Apr-Sep 2025)
4. AR aging report as of Sept 30, 2025
5. List of credit notes issued in H1
6. List of foreign currency transactions with FIRC/BRC status
7. Month-end revenue reconciliations (if prepared)
8. Customer contracts/rate cards for major clients

**TM3 Support:**
- Extract sales register from Tally
- Verify invoice numbering sequence
- Prepare revenue trend analysis by month
- Identify outliers and unusual transactions

### Phase 3: Control Testing (Week 3-5)

#### Test 1: Invoice Completeness and Sequencing
**Objective**: Ensure all sales are invoiced and no invoices are missing

**Procedure:**
1. Obtain invoice sequence from Tally (TM3)
2. Check for gaps in invoice numbering
3. For any gaps, obtain explanation and verify cancellation approval
4. Test invoice series for both BPO and Ticketing separately

**Sample**: 100% of invoice sequence (automated test via Tally)

**Workpaper**: H1-REV-01

**Expected Evidence:**
- Continuous invoice sequence or approved cancellations
- Finance Head approval for cancelled invoices

#### Test 2: Invoice Accuracy and Approval
**Objective**: Verify invoices are raised at correct rates and approved

**Procedure:**
1. Select sample of 30 invoices (stratified):
   - 15 BPO invoices (mix of clients and months)
   - 15 Ticketing invoices (mix of high and low value)
   - Ensure coverage of all 6 months
2. For each invoice:
   - Verify rate against contract/rate card
   - Recalculate invoice amount
   - Check for Finance Head review/approval (email/system log)
   - Verify GST calculation
   - Confirm invoice recorded in Tally correctly

**Sample Selection Criteria:**
- Top 5 customers by revenue (all invoices)
- Random selection for remaining
- Include month-end invoices (cut-off testing)

**Workpaper**: H1-REV-02 to H1-REV-04 (split by invoice type)

**Expected Evidence:**
- Signed contracts or approved rate cards
- Correct calculations
- Approval evidence (email, Tally approval log)

#### Test 3: Receipt Application and Bank Reconciliation
**Objective**: Ensure customer payments are properly identified and applied

**Procedure:**
1. Select sample of 20 customer receipts:
   - 10 domestic receipts
   - 10 foreign currency receipts
2. For each receipt:
   - Trace from bank statement to Tally entry
   - Verify receipt applied to correct invoice
   - For forex: Verify FIRC/BRC obtained and filed
   - Check Senior Accounts Executive validation
   - Verify timely recording (within 2 days of receipt)

**Sample Selection:**
- High-value receipts (>â‚¹5 lakhs)
- All forex receipts in H1 (if <10, else sample 10)
- Mix across all months

**Workpaper**: H1-REV-05 to H1-REV-06

**Expected Evidence:**
- Bank statement showing receipt
- Tally entry matching bank
- Invoice marked as paid
- FIRC/BRC for forex receipts
- Senior Accounts Executive sign-off

#### Test 4: Credit Notes and Refunds
**Objective**: Ensure credit notes are properly authorized

**Procedure:**
1. Obtain list of all credit notes in H1
2. If <10, test all; if >10, sample 10
3. For each credit note:
   - Verify reason (cancellation, rate correction, etc.)
   - Check authorization (Finance Head approval)
   - Verify original invoice details
   - Confirm refund processed (if applicable) with bank proof
   - Check GST reversal

**Workpaper**: H1-REV-07

**Expected Evidence:**
- Cancellation request or rate correction approval
- Finance Head authorization email
- Refund bank statement (if cash refund)

#### Test 5: Revenue Cut-off Testing
**Objective**: Ensure revenue recognized in correct period

**Procedure:**
1. Select last 10 invoices of September 2025
2. Select first 10 invoices of October 2025
3. For each:
   - Verify service delivery date
   - Confirm invoice date aligns with service period
   - Check if any Sept services billed in Oct (should be unbilled revenue)
   - Check if any Oct services billed in Sept (should be deferred)

**Workpaper**: H1-REV-08

**Expected Evidence:**
- Service delivery documentation (timesheets for BPO, booking dates for ticketing)
- Proper period allocation

#### Test 6: Unbilled and Deferred Revenue
**Objective**: Verify identification of unbilled/deferred revenue at H1 end

**Procedure:**
1. Inquire with Finance Manager about unbilled revenue process
2. Obtain unbilled revenue schedule as of Sept 30, 2025 (if prepared)
3. Test calculation for 5 items:
   - Verify service delivered but not invoiced
   - Check subsequent invoicing in Oct
4. Obtain deferred revenue schedule (if any)
5. Test 5 items:
   - Verify cash received for future services
   - Check service delivery in subsequent period

**Workpaper**: H1-REV-09

**Expected Evidence:**
- Unbilled revenue schedule with senior management approval
- Supporting documentation (timesheets, delivery notes)
- Subsequent invoices

#### Test 7: Accounts Receivable Aging and Collections
**Objective**: Assess AR management and collection effectiveness

**Procedure:**
1. Obtain AR aging as of Sept 30, 2025
2. Analyze aging buckets (0-30, 31-60, 61-90, >90 days)
3. For receivables >90 days:
   - Verify escalation to Finance Head (per SOP)
   - Check collection follow-up evidence
   - Assess need for bad debt provision
4. Test subsequent collections (Oct-Nov) for aged items

**Workpaper**: H1-REV-10

**Expected Evidence:**
- AR aging report
- Collection follow-up emails
- Finance Head review of aged items

#### Test 8: Segregation of Duties
**Objective**: Ensure proper segregation in revenue process

**Procedure:**
1. Document roles and responsibilities:
   - Who creates invoices?
   - Who approves invoices?
   - Who records receipts?
   - Who approves credit notes?
2. Check for any overlaps or conflicts
3. Verify oversight by Finance Head/Manager

**Workpaper**: H1-REV-11

**Expected Evidence:**
- Role matrix
- Tally user access rights
- Evidence of supervisory reviews

#### Test 9: System Controls - Tally
**Objective**: Verify Tally controls for revenue process

**Procedure (TM3 Lead):**
1. Check if invoices can be deleted without trace
2. Verify period locking for Apr-Sep months
3. Review Tally audit trail for any invoice modifications
4. Test if backdated entries are prevented after period lock

**Workpaper**: H1-REV-12 (TM3)

**Expected Evidence:**
- Tally audit trail report
- Period lock status for each month
- No unauthorized modifications

#### Test 10: Analytical Procedures
**Objective**: Identify anomalies requiring investigation

**Procedure (TM3 Lead):**
1. Monthly revenue trend analysis (Apr-Sep)
2. Revenue by customer (top 10 customers)
3. Revenue by stream (BPO vs Ticketing)
4. Gross margin analysis (if cost data available)
5. Compare to budget/prior year
6. Investigate significant variances (>20%)

**Workpaper**: H1-REV-13 (TM3)

**Expected Evidence:**
- Trend charts and variance analysis
- Management explanations for variances

### Phase 4: Findings Documentation (Week 6)

**Deliverables:**

### Phase 5: Reporting (Week 7-8)

- By month (ensure all 6 months covered)

- By customer (major customers fully covered)

### Judgmental Samples

## EXPECTED FINDINGS (Proactive Identification)

### Potential Issues to Watch For
1. **Invoice gaps**: Missing invoice numbers without proper cancellation
2. **Delayed FIRC**: Foreign receipts without timely FIRC documentation
3. **Unbilled revenue**: Services delivered in Sept but invoiced in Oct
4. **Aged receivables**: Long-outstanding amounts without collection efforts
5. **Period lock**: Months not locked allowing backdated entries
6. **Credit note approvals**: Missing Finance Head authorization
7. **Rate discrepancies**: Invoices not matching approved rates

### Good Practices to Note
1. Timely invoicing (within 2 days of service delivery)
2. Prompt receipt application (same day as bank receipt)
3. Regular AR aging review by management
4. Strong segregation of duties
5. Effective use of Tally controls
