## RISK ASSESSMENT

### High Risk Areas
- **Capex vs Expense misclassification**: Manipulation of profits
- **Unauthorized capex**: Spending beyond budget
- **Missing assets**: Theft or loss not recorded
- **Incorrect depreciation**: Impact on P&L

### Medium Risk Areas
- Delayed capitalization
- Physical verification gaps
- Asset register vs GL reconciliation
- Disposal approvals

### Low Risk Areas
- Routine depreciation for existing assets
- Standard asset tagging

## METHODOLOGY

### Phase 1: Planning & Process Understanding (Week 1)

**Activities:**
1. Review Fixed Assets SOP (if available) or FSCP sections on assets
2. Conduct walkthrough with:
   - Finance Manager (asset accounting)
   - Finance Executive (asset register maintenance)
   - Admin Team (asset procurement, physical custody)
3. Understand capitalization policy:
   - Capitalization threshold
   - Useful life by asset category
   - Depreciation method (straight-line per SOP)
4. Review Annual Operating Plan for capex budget
5. Identify key controls

**Deliverables:**
- Process understanding memo
- Capitalization policy summary
- Control identification matrix

### Phase 2: Data Collection (Week 1-2)

**Data Requests:**
1. Fixed Asset Register (FAR) as of Mar 31, 2026
2. List of all asset additions in FY25-26
3. List of asset disposals/write-offs in FY25-26 (if any)
4. Depreciation schedule for FY25-26
5. Purchase orders and invoices for asset additions
6. Capex approval documents
7. Physical verification report (if conducted)
8. Asset tagging records
9. GL ledgers for:
   - Fixed Assets (by category)
   - Accumulated Depreciation
   - Depreciation Expense
   - CWIP (if any)
10. Insurance policy covering assets
11. Capex budget from AOP

**TM3 Support:**
- Extract asset-related ledgers from Tally
- Prepare capex trend analysis

### Phase 3: Control Testing (Week 2-4)

#### Test FA1: Capital Expenditure Authorization
**Objective**: Verify capex approvals per authorization matrix

**Procedure:**
1. Obtain list of all asset additions in FY25-26
2. Stratify by value:
   - High value (>₹5 lakhs)
   - Medium value (₹1-5 lakhs)
   - Low value (<₹1 lakh)
3. Sample selection:
   - All high-value additions
   - 50% of medium-value
   - 20% of low-value
   - Minimum total sample: 15 assets
4. For each sampled asset:
   - Verify purchase requisition
   - Check capex budget/AOP comparison
   - Verify approval per authorization matrix:
     * Admin Head for routine capex
     * MD/Director for high-value or off-budget
   - Check approval date (before PO issuance)
   - Verify business justification documented

**Workpaper**: Q4-FAR-01 to Q4-FAR-02

**Expected Evidence:**
- Purchase requisitions
- Capex approval emails/forms
- Budget comparison worksheets
- MD/Director approvals for high-value

#### Test FA2: Asset Capitalization - Criteria and Timing
**Objective**: Ensure only qualifying expenditures are capitalized

**Procedure:**
1. For sampled assets (from Test FA1):
   - Verify expenditure meets capitalization criteria:
     * Cost >capitalization threshold (per policy)
     * Useful life >1 year
     * Provides future economic benefit
   - Check capitalization amount includes:
     * Invoice cost
     * Installation/setup costs
     * Any other directly attributable costs
   - Verify capitalization date (when asset ready for use)
   - Check Finance Manager review of capitalization entry
2. Review repairs & maintenance expense account:
   - Scan for large amounts (>₹50,000)
   - Assess if any should have been capitalized
   - Investigate 5 largest items

**Workpaper**: Q4-FAR-03 to Q4-FAR-04

**Expected Evidence:**
- Asset invoices
- Installation/setup invoices
- Capitalization policy
- Finance Manager review sign-off
- Repairs & maintenance ledger

#### Test FA3: Asset Classification and Useful Life
**Objective**: Verify assets classified correctly with appropriate useful life

**Procedure:**
1. For sampled assets:
   - Verify asset category in FAR (IT, Furniture, etc.)
   - Check useful life assigned
   - Compare with company policy
   - Compare with Schedule II of Companies Act (if applicable)
   - Verify consistency with prior years
2. Check if any changes in useful life during the year:
   - If yes, verify board approval (if required)
   - Assess impact on depreciation

**Workpaper**: Q4-FAR-05

**Expected Evidence:**
- Fixed Asset Register
- Capitalization policy
- Schedule II reference
- Board approval for any changes

#### Test FA4: Depreciation Calculation
**Objective**: Verify depreciation calculated correctly

**Procedure:**
1. Understand depreciation method (straight-line per SOP)
2. For sampled assets:
   - Recalculate annual depreciation:
     * (Cost - Residual Value) / Useful Life
   - For additions, verify pro-rata depreciation from date of use
   - For disposals, verify depreciation up to disposal date
   - Compare recalculation with FAR
3. Test monthly depreciation posting:
   - Select 3 months (Oct, Jan, Mar)
   - Verify depreciation journal entry in Tally
   - Reconcile with depreciation schedule
   - Check Finance Manager review
4. Verify full-year depreciation:
   - Total depreciation expense in GL
   - Total per depreciation schedule
   - Reconcile any differences

**Workpaper**: Q4-FAR-06 to Q4-FAR-08

**Expected Evidence:**
- Depreciation schedule
- Recalculation worksheets
- Tally depreciation journal entries
- Finance Manager review

#### Test FA5: Asset Disposals and Write-offs
**Objective**: Ensure disposals properly approved and recorded

**Procedure:**
1. Obtain list of all disposals/write-offs in FY25-26
2. Test all if <5, else sample 5
3. For each disposal:
   - Verify reason (obsolete, damaged, sold)
   - Check management approval (Finance Head/MD)
   - Verify asset removed from FAR
   - Recalculate gain/loss on disposal:
     * Sale proceeds (if sold)
     * Less: Net book value (Cost - Accumulated Depreciation)
     * Equals: Gain/(Loss)
   - Verify accounting entry in Tally
   - Check if sale proceeds received (if sold)
   - Verify GST implications handled (if applicable)

**Workpaper**: Q4-FAR-09

**Expected Evidence:**
- Disposal approval documents
- Sale invoices (if sold)
- Gain/loss calculation
- Tally disposal entries
- Bank receipt (if sold)

#### Test FA6: Physical Verification of Assets
**Objective**: Assess physical verification process and results

**Procedure:**
1. Inquire about physical verification policy (annual, biennial?)
2. Check if physical verification conducted in FY25-26:
   - If yes:
     * Obtain physical verification report
     * Review verification date and team
     * Check assets verified vs FAR
     * Identify any discrepancies (missing, unrecorded)
     * Verify discrepancy investigation and resolution
     * Check management approval for write-offs
   - If no:
     * Note as observation
     * Recommend conducting verification
3. Perform sample physical verification (if feasible):
   - Select 10 assets from FAR
   - Physically locate and verify:
     * Asset exists
     * Asset tag matches FAR
     * Asset in working condition
     * Asset location matches FAR
   - Document any issues

**Workpaper**: Q4-FAR-10 to Q4-FAR-11

**Expected Evidence:**
- Physical verification report
- Discrepancy resolution documentation
- Write-off approvals
- Audit team verification notes and photos

#### Test FA7: Asset Tagging and Tracking
**Objective**: Ensure assets are tagged and tracked

**Procedure:**
1. Review asset tagging policy
2. For sampled assets (from physical verification):
   - Verify asset tag affixed
   - Check tag number matches FAR
   - Verify tag is durable and legible
3. Review FAR for completeness:
   - Asset description
   - Serial number (if applicable)
   - Location
   - Custodian/user
   - Tag number
4. Check if any assets without tags:
   - Investigate reason
   - Recommend tagging

**Workpaper**: Q4-FAR-12

**Expected Evidence:**
- Asset tagging policy
- FAR with tag numbers
- Physical verification notes

#### Test FA8: Fixed Asset Register vs General Ledger Reconciliation
**Objective**: Ensure FAR reconciles with GL

**Procedure:**
1. Obtain FAR summary as of Mar 31, 2026:
   - Gross block by category
   - Accumulated depreciation by category
   - Net block by category
2. Obtain GL balances as of Mar 31, 2026:
   - Fixed Assets ledgers by category
   - Accumulated Depreciation ledgers by category
3. Reconcile FAR to GL:
   - Opening balance (Apr 1, 2025)
   - Add: Additions during the year
   - Less: Disposals during the year
   - Closing balance (Mar 31, 2026)
4. Investigate any differences
5. Verify Finance Executive prepared reconciliation
6. Check Finance Manager review

**Workpaper**: Q4-FAR-13

**Expected Evidence:**
- FAR summary
- GL ledgers
- Reconciliation worksheet
- Finance Manager review

#### Test FA9: Capital Work-in-Progress (CWIP)
**Objective**: Verify CWIP is properly tracked and transferred

**Procedure:**
1. Obtain CWIP balance as of Mar 31, 2026
2. If CWIP exists:
   - Review nature of projects
   - Verify costs accumulated are capital in nature
   - Check project status (ongoing or completed)
   - For completed projects:
     * Verify transfer to fixed assets
     * Check depreciation started
   - For ongoing projects:
     * Verify no depreciation charged
     * Assess if project genuinely ongoing or should be capitalized
3. If no CWIP:
   - Verify no capital projects were ongoing

**Workpaper**: Q4-FAR-14

**Expected Evidence:**
- CWIP ledger
- Project status reports
- Transfer entries (if completed)

#### Test FA10: Capex vs Revenue Expenditure Cut-off
**Objective**: Ensure proper cut-off at year-end

**Procedure:**
1. Review last 10 days of March 2026:
   - Scan repairs & maintenance expenses
   - Scan other expense accounts for potential capex
2. Review first 10 days of April 2026:
   - Scan for any asset additions that should be in March
3. Investigate any large or unusual items
4. Assess if cut-off is appropriate

**Workpaper**: Q4-FAR-15

**Expected Evidence:**
- Expense ledgers for late March
- Asset additions in early April
- Investigation notes

#### Test FA11: Asset Insurance Coverage
**Objective**: Verify assets are adequately insured

**Procedure:**
1. Obtain insurance policy covering assets
2. Verify coverage:
   - Types of assets covered
   - Sum insured
   - Policy period (should cover FY25-26)
3. Compare sum insured with FAR net block
4. Assess adequacy of coverage
5. Check premium payment

**Workpaper**: Q4-FAR-16

**Expected Evidence:**
- Insurance policy document
- Premium payment receipt
- Coverage adequacy assessment

#### Test FA12: Analytical Procedures
**Objective**: Identify anomalies requiring investigation

**Procedure (TM3 Support):**
1. Capex trend analysis:
   - Monthly capex in FY25-26
   - Compare to budget
   - Compare to prior year
2. Depreciation analysis:
   - Depreciation as % of gross block
   - Compare to prior year
   - Assess reasonableness
3. Asset turnover ratios (if relevant)
4. Investigate significant variances

**Workpaper**: Q4-FAR-17 (TM3)

**Expected Evidence:**
- Trend charts
- Variance analysis
- Management explanations
